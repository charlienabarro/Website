Table Tennis

Up-and-coming talents

  1. The Lebrun twins
    • There are many young table tennis coming through the ranks, however, none more than the Lebrun twins from France. At just 18 and 20, the lebrun twins are already some of the best players in the world with the pair taking home the bronze medal for France at the latest olympics in the team event, making it just the second time in history that france have taken home a team medal. Felix, the younger of the two, even managed to get a bronze medal in the singles event, making him the youngest player in history to win a medal at the olympics in singles.

  2. Benyamin Faraji
    • Whilst he was relatively unknown and thought of as a future rising star who may make an impact on the mens game in future years, Faraji caught the attention of most table tennis fan when he had an unprecedented win,at just 14 years old, against the world number 1 (Wang Chuqin) at the 2024 Asian Championship. The win has brought the player int the limelight and with a 3-2 loss to another Chinese star, Faraji has shown he has a huge future ahead of himself.
